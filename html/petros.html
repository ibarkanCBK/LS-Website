Shamm H. Petros, Partner - Learning & Evaluation

As a refugee from Eritrea, Shamm Petros, is committed to supporting the psychological well-being and development of migrant children across the world. She has six years of experience conducting extensive research for program development, leading project design, securing eight million USD in funding and engineering tools for various refugee protection and education programs across the USA, Kenya, Ethiopia, Uganda and Iraq.

Additionally, Ms. Petros has provided direct clinical services to African-Americans and forced migrants affected by complex and sexual trauma. Her unique experience in international refugee relief services has prepared her to provide direct clinical services and lead the design and execution of trauma-informed, culturally relevant psychosocial interventions for various populations of concern.

Ms. Petros hopes to study the effects of trauma on children and families, in order to prepare institutions such as schools, hospitals, community based organizations and government agencies to provide adequate counseling and education support for vulnerable youth such as refugees, forced migrants, children involved in the juvenile system, survivors of trauma, and other vulnerable populations. Currently, Shamm is the Clinical Research Coordinator of the Racial Empowerment Collaborative at the University of Pennsylvania, leading the delivery, monitoring and evaluation of a school-based cognitive-behavioral trauma intervention.
